# Faulty Nvidia H100 GPUs and HBM3 Memory Caused Half of Llama 3 Training Failures

**Source URL:** https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/faulty-nvidia-h100-gpus-and-hbm3-memory-caused-half-of-the-failures-during-llama-3-training-one-failure-every-three-hours-for-metas-16384-gpu-training-cluster

**Date Accessed:** 2026-03-11

---

**Author:** Anton Shilov
**Publication:** Tom's Hardware
**Date:** July 27, 2024

## Key Findings

According to the article, Meta experienced significant hardware reliability challenges during its Llama 3 model training on a massive 16,384 GPU cluster. "One failure every three hours" occurred during the training process, with faulty Nvidia H100 GPUs and HBM3 (High Bandwidth Memory 3) components responsible for approximately half of all system failures.

## Technical Context

The article highlights that despite these substantial failure rates, Meta had implemented mitigation strategies to manage the reliability issues. The scale of Meta's training infrastructure—operating thousands of premium GPUs simultaneously—amplified the impact of individual component failures, making hardware reliability a critical concern for large-scale AI model development.

## Implications

This reporting underscores the practical challenges facing organizations deploying cutting-edge AI hardware at massive scale. The frequency of failures reveals the importance of redundancy, fault tolerance, and proactive hardware validation in large-scale machine learning operations.

The article demonstrates that even with premium enterprise-grade components, hardware defects remain a significant operational concern in modern AI training infrastructure.
